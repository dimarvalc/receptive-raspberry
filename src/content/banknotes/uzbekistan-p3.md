### Historical Catalog Verification Log
This crisp 5 Süm paper currency instrument represents a vital milestone in Central Asian monetary history. Issued immediately following Uzbekistan’s declaration of sovereignty from the Soviet Union, this initial banknotes series was vital in stabilizing domestic commerce during the complex transition to full economic autonomy.

### Surface Engraving & Numismatic Details
The banknote features a distinctive multi-color layout utilizing horizontal bands of yellow, pale teal, and sky blue on the face, shifting to a solid, monocromatic violet/purple palette on the reverse canvas. It has a compact physical size of 144 × 71 mm.
* **Obverse Face**: Showcases the newly minted State Emblem of the *Republic of Uzbekistan* on the left, displaying the wings of the legendary Huma bird flanking a rising sun landscape. The Cyrillic text banner reads *ЎЗБЕКИСТОН ДАВЛАТ БАНКИ* (State Bank of Uzbekistan). Stamped with a clean black serial security registry string (**EA 46499959**).
* **Reverse Backing**: Displays a beautiful architectural engraving portraying the historic *Sher-Dor Madrasah* at Registan Square in Samarkand, highlighting its massive fluted dome and mosaic arches.

### Security Elements & Variety Identification
The fine-line background pattern utilizes interlocking Soviet-era security lines and multi-directional background underprints to impede reproduction. The reverse panel features four intricate geometric decorative rosettes that frame the numeric face values (`5`), matching traditional Islamic mosaic aesthetics.
