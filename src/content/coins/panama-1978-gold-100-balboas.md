### Numismatic Historical Log

#### Physical & Strike Assessment
This stunning gold piece features an exceptional high-relief strike displaying full original mint luster. The official obverse is anchored by the intricate Panamanian National Coat of Arms, flanked cleanly by the purity markers `900/1000 ORO FINO` and the small `FM` mint mark. The reverse bears the graceful rendering of three Dove Orchids wrapped inside a circular perimeter border. Fields are pristine, showcasing deep mirror-like qualities characteristic of a premier uncirculated strike.

#### Historical Context & Lineage
Minted under special government contract by the private Franklin Mint in the United States, this 1978 issue holds a limited collector mintage of only 6,086 pieces. Instead of utilizing a traditional dove bird, Panama uniquely opted to feature its national flower—the *Peristeria elata* or Dove Orchid—to visually represent the coin's core theme, `PAZ Y PROGRESO` (Peace and Progress). Strikingly preserved, this specimen remains a top-tier premium asset within the modern Latin American precious metal ledger.
