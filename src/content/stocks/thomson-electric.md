### Certificate Issuance Registry Log
This historical security instrument features an intricate, geometric **Green border frame** engraved by *Thos. W. Stationers, Boston*. Incorporated under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, the certificate represents capital stock holdings valued at a par value of $20 per share, out of a highly specific total corporate authorized volume capitalization of 9,602 shares yielding a corporate value limit of $192,040.

This specific certificate (#85) was hand-issued to *Adelbert Stephenson* for exactly **4 shares** on March 22, 1910, in Lynn, Mass., bearing a crisp embossed corporate seal in the lower-left corner highlighting the 1910 operational tracking timeline.

### Legal Cancellation & Archival Condition
The certificate represents a complete corporate lifecycle, terminated exactly a decade after printing:
* **Security Voiding**: Distinctive star-shaped safety cancellation punches are driven directly through the executive signature fields of the Treasurer and President. 
* **Corporate Status**: A faded purple ink overprint reading **"CANCELLED / TRUST"** runs diagonally across the face, matched by a mechanical margin stamp recording the final treasury redemption on **December 14, 1920**.

### Reverse Endorsement, Probate Administration & Philatelic Variants
The backing canvas holds rich, extensive legal histories documenting an estate liquidation following the death of the original holder:
* **Probate Court Intervention**: Detailed manuscript notations trace an authorization by the *Essex County Probate Court* dated July 26, 1920 (Registry Case #135605), appointing *Charles H. Stephenson* as Administrator of the estate of *Adelbert Stephenson*. The transfer assigns the 4 shares over to Charles H. Stephenson.
* **Signature Guarantees**: The legal transfer proxy is witnessed by *John B. Newhall* and explicitly verified via a stamped signature guarantee block from the *Essex Trust Company of Lynn, Mass.*
* **Fiscal Stamps Added**: To legally finalize the transfer tax transaction on December 14, 1920, a 2-cent red US Internal Revenue Documentary stamp and a 2-cent orange Commonwealth of Massachusetts Stock Transfer Tax stamp were affixed.
