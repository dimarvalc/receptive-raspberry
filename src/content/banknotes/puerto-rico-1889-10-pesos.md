### Banknote Security Feature Review

* **Steel-Plate Intaglio Vignettes:** Features world-class master engraving detailing from the American Bank Note Company. The incredibly tight cross-hatching layout lines on the central `10` shield and outer text ribbons serve as an advanced anti-copy safeguard.
* **Complex Geometric Reverse:** The reverse utilizes a stunning, deeply saturated brown-sepia ink configuration displaying massive geometric circular lathe matrices and radial lace patterns extending to the corners.
* **Specimen Protection Ledger:** Overprinted with a bold red lowercase `SPECIMEN` marker in the lower focal area, flanked by zeroed serial tracking arrays and four clean cancellation holes stamped along the bottom margin lines.

### Catalog Historical Narrative

#### Late Colonial Monetary Autonomy
Authorized by a special Royal Decree issued in Madrid in May 1888, *El Banco Español de Puerto Rico* was established to unify and stabilize the volatile local economy of the island province. This iconic 1889 emission series marks the first true official banknotes printed expressly for widespread island circulation, moving away from relying entirely on foreign coinages or emergency local scrips. It captures the artistic peak of late 19th-century Spanish colonial wealth production.

#### Detailed Iconography
The obverse left side encloses a beautiful circular cameo framework showcasing the *Agnus Dei* (Lamb of God) carrying the cross-staff banner over crashing ocean waves, flanked by the colonial tracking letters `F` and `I`. The right quadrant exhibits an impressive, beautifully executed maritime scene depicting two sailors standing on a rocky Caribbean coastline—one actively spying passing tall ships through a heavy brass telescope. The reverse is dominated by a majestic central seal containing the full crowned Spanish Royal Coat of Arms, boldly encircled by the institution’s official imperial title frame.
