### Historical Catalog Verification Log
This high-denomination 1000 Escudos banknote represents a monumental political transition in Southeast Africa. Originally engraved and printed by *Thomas De La Rue & Co. in London* for the colonial Portuguese administration, these unissued legal tenders were hand-stamped with a heavy black **"BANCO DE MOÇAMBIQUE"** overprint following national independence to serve as temporary emergency transitional currency.

### Surface Engraving & Numismatic Details
The banknote boasts a highly technical, multi-layered color matrix utilizing shades of jade green, deep olive, and pastel yellow background geometric underprints, spanning a large physical canvas size of 170 × 84 mm.
* **Obverse Face**: Features the detailed portrait engraving of Portuguese navigator and naval aviator *Carlos Viegas Gago Coutinho* at the left quadrant. The center contains the official Portuguese colonial coat of arms, partially intersected by a crisp, black ink-stamped overprint authorizing its transfer to the newly formed post-independence state banking system. Stamped with a clean black serial security registry string (**C 088708**).
* **Reverse Backing**: Showcases a beautifully executed mechanical engraving capturing aviators Gago Coutinho and Sacadura Cabral inside the open cockpit of their biplane during their legendary 1922 South Atlantic crossing. The lower-left margin retains the original corporate ship seal of the *Banco Nacional Ultramarino*.

### Security Elements & Variety Identification
The note incorporates advanced banknote printing technology, including complex micro-lettering lines, overlapping multi-color security patterns, and a prominent circular **watermark window** on the upper right edge displaying a historical silhouette portrait profile. Intricate rosette guilloché scrollwork frames the four corners, safely anchoring the numeric denomination values (`1000`).
