### Historical Catalog Verification Log
This fractional 2 Kak (0.2 Riel) paper currency instrument represents a critical fiscal milestone issued by the central banking authority following the liberation of Phnom Penh in early 1979. Printed during a period of massive national reconstruction, this emergency series was vital in re-introducing a standardized monetary trade system to the country.

### Surface Engraving & Numismatic Details
The note features a rich dual-tone deep forest green and soft cream/terracotta sunburst background matrix layout, measuring a compact physical footprint size of 107 × 53 mm.
* **Obverse Face**: Showcases the official State Emblem of the *People's Republic of Kampuchea* prominently at the center, highlighting the architectural silhouette of the Angkor Wat temple towers above an industrial drive cogwheel. The Khmer text header translates directly to *Bank of the People of Kampuchea*. Stamped with a striking high-contrast red 8-digit serial security ledger string (**បម 8554539**).
* **Reverse Backing**: Displays a detailed agrarian engraving portraying three traditional Cambodian women hand-planting young rice crops within a mirrored water terrace. A traditional agricultural plowing scene with two domestic water buffalos is active in the upper right background under a canopy of indigenous palm silhouettes.

### Security Elements & Variety Identification
The fine-line background utilizes complex geometric multi-layered guilloché rosette security shields on the corners to inhibit fraudulent photo-mechanical replication. Corner numeric denominations alternate between indigenous Khmer numbers (`๐.๒`) on the front corners and standard Western Arabic numerals (`0.2`) on the upper-right reverse margins.
