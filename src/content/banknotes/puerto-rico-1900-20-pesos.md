### Banknote Security Feature Review

* **Intaglio Plate Engraving:** Produced via razor-sharp monochrome steel-plate intaglio printing by the American Bank Note Company, New York. The complex lathe work geometric borders are highly advanced for the era to prevent early photographic counterfeiting.
* **Security Underprints:** Features a delicate, multi-toned geometric guilloche security mesh underprint layer that transitions softly behind the primary text and central vignette.
* **Planchet Hole Punching:** This pristine specimen note utilizes four distinct circular cancellation hole punches along the bottom margin to officially invalidate the asset from financial circulation while retaining original paper integrity.

### Catalog Historical Narrative

#### Post-War Monetary Transition
This phenomenal bank note represents one of the most historically significant provisional paper money emissions in the history of Caribbean numismatics. Struck shortly after the conclusion of the Spanish-American War of 1898, it captures the raw geopolitical pivot point as Puerto Rico transitioned from a Spanish colonial economy to an American territory. To maintain local business stability, the newly established "Banco de Puerto Rico" issued these notes textually dual-denominated in both `VEINTE PESOS` and `TWENTY DOLLARS (U.S. C.Y.)`.

#### Visual Iconography & Registry
The face profile centers around a beautifully engraved seated neoclassical female allegory holding an absolute bounty of local agricultural goods and tropical fruits, overlooking a historic colonial harbor landscape. The deep blue reverse displays the highly celebrated official crest of Puerto Rico: the *Agnus Dei* (Lamb of God) standing confidently with a cross-staff banner over ocean waves, framed inside an intricate lace border format. Carrying a zero-serial configuration (`Nº 00000`) and a bold red `SPECIMEN` overprint stamp, this asset is an exceptionally high-tier museum preservation centerpiece.
