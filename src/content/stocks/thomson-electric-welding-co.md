### Certificate Issuance Registry Log
This historical security instrument features an intricate, geometric **Green border frame** engraved by *Thos. W. Stationers, Boston*. Incorporated under the laws of the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, the certificate represents capital stock holdings valued at a par value of $20 per share, out of a total corporate authorized volume capitalization of 9,609 shares. 

This specific certificate (#85) was hand-issued to *Adelbert Stephenson* for exactly **4 shares** on March 22, 1910, bearing the crisp embossed corporate seal in the lower-left corner.

### Legal Cancellation & Archival Condition
The certificate was officially voided on **December 14, 1920**, as confirmed by the matching manuscript cancellation ink on the reverse layer. 
* **Security Voiding**: Star-shaped safety cancellation punches are driven directly through the executive signature fields of the Treasurer and President. 
* **Corporate Status**: A faded purple ink overprint reading **"CANCELLED / TRUST"** runs diagonally across the face, indicating trust administration or asset liquidation.

### Reverse Endorsement & Philatelic Variants
The backing canvas holds rich fiscal histories documenting the assignment and transfer of the stock volume:
* **Documentary Revenue Stamp**: A 2-cent red US Internal Revenue Documentary stamp is affixed at the lower-left.
* **Transfer Tax Stamp**: A 2-cent orange Commonwealth of Massachusetts Stock Transfer Tax stamp is affixed adjacent to the documentary issue.
* Both fiscal stamps feature matching star-shaped cancels and are hand-signed and dated *Dec 14, 1920* to authorize the legal execution of the tax assignment. The signature is guaranteed by the *Essex Trust Company of Lynn, Mass.*
