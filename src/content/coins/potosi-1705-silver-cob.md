### Numismatic Historical Log

#### Physical & Strike Assessment
This hand-struck silver cob exhibits a fascinating, irregular planchet with excellent structural thickness. The obverse features the traditional Jerusalem Cross layout containing alternating castles and lions in the quarters, framed by a partial peripheral inscription. The reverse displays the historic Pillars of Hercules rising out of stylized ocean waves, inscribed with the motto `PLV SVL TRA` (Plus Ultra). A clean, contemporary holed piercing sits at the 12 o'clock position relative to the reverse pillars, representing authentic historical use as a wearable monetary asset. 

#### Historical Context & Lineage
Produced at the high-altitude Imperial Mint of Potosí (`P`) during the turbulent transition years of the War of the Spanish Succession under King Philip V. Hand-struck by indigenous and enslaved artisans utilizing heavy mallets on crude silver planchets sheared from poured bars, cob coinage was designed for rapid bullion export rather than aesthetic perfection. The bold, centrally struck date digits `705` nestled between the two vertical pillars make this an exceptionally well-identified and highly desirable artifact of early global maritime commerce.
