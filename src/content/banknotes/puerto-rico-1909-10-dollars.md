### Banknote Security Feature Review

* **Dual-Color Intaglio:** The face features a highly sophisticated dark olive-green primary intaglio plate overlaying a vibrant orange-red background security medallion stamp on the right.
* **Micro-Guilloche Framework:** The reverse utilizes intricate, mathematically generated geometric lathe work patterns tinted in a rich brown sepia ink tone to counter counter-photographic replicating processes.
* **Specimen Modifications:** Features zeroed-out serial matrices (`Nº 00000`), dual punch-canceled red `SPECIMEN` stamps on the lower border, and four clean cancellation holes punched straight through the base text line.

### Catalog Historical Narrative

#### Institutional Currency Evolution
By 1909, the island's banking infrastructure shifted fully under the American monetary umbrella, legally cementing the US Dollar as the default medium of exchange. While the financial entity retained its dual billing title (`BANK OF PORTO RICO` / `BANCO DE PUERTO RICO`), this 1909 emission formally dropped the "Peso" face value terminology seen on the 1900 series. Instead, the asset represents a clean bilingual dollar standard displaying `TEN DOLLARS` flanked directly over `DIEZ DÓLARES`.

#### Historic Engravings
The obverse left panel features a remarkably detailed steel-engraved portrait bust of Juan Ponce de León, the historic first governor of Puerto Rico, sporting an ornate Renaissance-era breastplate and plumed explorer cap. To the right, a striking red seal displays Puerto Rico's early colonial coat of arms featuring the iconic lamb. The reverse centers around a gorgeous circular cameo framework enclosing a left-facing profile of a classicized Liberty allegory sporting a Phrygian cap. Flanking the vignette are explicit historical references tracing the charter's origins from Madrid (1888) to its subsequent legal ratification in Washington, D.C. (1900).
